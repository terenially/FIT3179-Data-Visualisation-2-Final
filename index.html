What primary energy sources power our world?


Primary energy sources are natural resources that are directly harvested or
extracted from the environment to generate energy.

These sources include fossil fuels (such as coal, oil, and natural gas),
renewable sources (such as sunlight, wind, and water), and nuclear materials.






Primary energy sources serve as the foundation for electricity generation,
transportation, and various industrial processes, playing a crucial role in
powering our daily lives and fueling economic activities.

Explore the world's energy landscape


See how different countries embrace primary energy consumption.




Countries with high energy consumption per capita often have a larger carbon footprint.


Larger carbon footprint refers to a higher total amount of greenhouse gases
, particularly carbon dioxide (CO2).


By reducing energy consumption per person, nations can
contribute significantly to global efforts to mitigate climate change.
